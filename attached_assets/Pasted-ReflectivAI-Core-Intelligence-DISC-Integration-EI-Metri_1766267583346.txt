PART 1 — CORE DNA: SIGNAL INTELLIGENCE (NON-NEGOTIABLE)
Canonical Definition (Do Not Rewrite)

Signal Intelligence is a working, non-canonical construct used within ReflectivAI to describe the ability to:

Notice, interpret, and respond appropriately to observable signals—verbal, conversational, engagement-based, and contextual—during professional interactions.

“Intelligence” refers to discernment, not cleverness.
The emphasis is on separating signal from noise in real time.

Signal Intelligence is not a validated behavioral science framework and must never be positioned as such.

Valid Signal Inputs (Strictly Enforced)

Signal Intelligence may operate only on observable interaction signals, including:

Verbal signals (tone shifts, pacing, certainty vs hesitation)

Conversational signals (deflection, repetition, topic avoidance)

Engagement signals (silence, responsiveness changes, abrupt closure)

Contextual / organizational signals (urgency cues, alignment language, stakeholder presence or absence)

You MUST NOT:

Infer emotional state

Infer personality or intent

Diagnose motivation

Assign permanent traits

All outputs must be framed as interpretive hypotheses, not truths.

PART 2 — DISC®: OPTIONAL, TRADEMARKED BEHAVIORAL INTEGRATION
Positioning Rules (Mandatory)

DISC is a trademarked behavioral communication model.

Within ReflectivAI:

DISC is never treated as Emotional Intelligence

DISC is never treated as proprietary

DISC is never inferred automatically by AI

DISC may be used only if:

Explicitly enabled by the client

Explicitly selected/configured by the user

Clearly labeled as a Behavioral Communication Model (DISC® or DISC-style)

Approved framing:

“Optional Behavioral Communication Lens (DISC®)”

Prohibited framing:

“DISC-based intelligence”

“DISC EQ”

“DISC-driven psychology”

DISC Functional Boundaries

DISC may:

Influence language style suggestions

Inform communication framing options

Act as a user-selected lens

DISC may NOT:

Override Signal Intelligence

Determine Influence Drivers

Drive scoring, prediction, or profiling

Appear as a default dropdown

PART 4 — EMOTIONAL INTELLIGENCE (EI): TERMINOLOGY & INTEGRITY
EI Definition (ReflectivAI-Specific, Defensible)

Within ReflectivAI, Emotional Intelligence refers to the user’s demonstrated ability to:

Accurately perceive signals from others

Interpret those signals appropriately in context

Regulate their response

Adapt communication in a way that preserves trust, clarity, and credibility

EI is treated as a demonstrated capability, not a personality trait.

Approved EI Metrics (Consistent Language)

EI metrics must use ability-based, observable language.

Approved examples:

Empathy Accuracy
How accurately the user recognized and responded to relevant signals.

Clarity & Alignment
How well the user’s response aligned to the other party’s expressed needs, constraints, or decision logic.

Adaptive Communication
How effectively the user adjusted their approach based on observed signals.

Discovery Depth
How well the user validated assumptions and surfaced meaningful information.

Avoid:

“Emotional awareness score”

“Personality fit”

“Emotional type”

PART 5 — EI SCORING / INDEX (USER-FRIENDLY & EXPLAINABLE)
Positioning

EI scoring in ReflectivAI is a Reflective Index, not a psychological assessment.

It represents:

“How consistently a user demonstrates emotionally intelligent behaviors in observable interactions.”

Calculation Principles (High Level)

EI scores must:

Be derived from observable behaviors and responses

Reflect pattern consistency, not one-off moments

Be explainable in plain language

Example framing (not math-heavy):

“Your EI score reflects how often your responses matched the signals present in the conversation.”

User-Facing Explanation (Required)

Every EI score must include:

What signals were observed

What response choices were made

Where alignment was strong

Where adaptation could improve

No opaque scoring.
No hidden weighting.
